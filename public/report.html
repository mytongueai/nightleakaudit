Key findings
FINDING 01
Zero practices offered online booking
Not a single practice mentioned online booking in their after-hours voicemail or IVR. Patients had no way to self-serve.
FINDING 02
82% hit a dead-end voicemail
No routing, no alternative, no way for the patient to book, pay, or get information. Just "leave a message."
FINDING 03
Only 18% mentioned a callback option
The rest gave no indication the patient would hear back. No timeframe, no promise, no next step.
FINDING 04
Major metros went completely dark
San Jose, NYC, San Francisco, Sacramento. Every practice in these cities went to voicemail or IVR after 5pm. The one live answer was in Los Angeles.
The pattern

Dental practices invest heavily in marketing to make the phone ring. Google Ads. Mailers. Social media. Referral programs.

Then at 5:01pm, they turn off the lights.

32% of all dental calls happen outside business hours. These aren't tire-kickers. These are patients in pain, patients comparing options after work, patients whose insurance just changed. They're ready to book.

Every voicemail is a coin flip. The patient either calls back tomorrow, or calls the next practice on Google. 75% of the time, they call the next practice.

Methodology
  • Calls placed between 5pm-8pm local time, April 15-17, 2026
  • All numbers sourced from public business listings (NPI Registry, Google Business)
  • AI-assisted calling system recorded outcome only (voicemail/live/IVR)
  • No patient data was collected or simulated
  • No practice names are published in this report
  • Practices were not notified in advance
